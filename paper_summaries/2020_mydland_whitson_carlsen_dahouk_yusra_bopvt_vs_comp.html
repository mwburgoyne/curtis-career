Black-Oil and Compositional Reservoir Simulation of Gas-Based EOR in Tight Unconventionals

Context

Fluid-model-comparison companion to the same-team SPE-200396 mechanism paper. Where SPE-200396 establishes what HnP and F2F are, this paper quantifies whether the standard black-oil PVT (BOPVT) reservoir-simulation fluid model is fit for purpose against the compositional (COMP) reference. All simulations on a planar-fracture symmetry-element model in Sensor (Coats Engineering) using a tuned 11-pseudo-component PR EOS for the field-wide Eagle Ford fluid suite (black oil, volatile oil, near-critical oil, near-critical gas condensate, gas condensate) and four injection gases (C2-enriched, lean-processed, separator, IG composition). PhazeComp (Zick) drives all PVT calculations. Five reservoir fluids crossed with multiple injection gases and multiple cases (single-cell DP HnP, well-scale HnP, two-layer HnP, two-phase-saturated HnP, F2F) map where BOPVT works and where it fails.

Key Contributions

  • BOPVT systematically overpredicts HnP recovery via static rs(p). The single BOPVT table assumes 1/rs(p) is invariant throughout the HnP process; the COMP reference shows rs exhibits severe hysteresis with each injection cycle as the matrix oil becomes heavier and harder to vaporise. Diagnostics: saturation-pressure decoupling (BOPVT ps collapses to ~1500 psia by cycle 5 while COMP ps climbs to 9930 psia); and the η(tep) cycle-efficiency table (BOPVT stays at 0.55+ while COMP drops to under 0.01). Overprediction can reach 10-20 percentage points of OOIP after several cycles.
  • Two-part BOPVT table generation with incremental-swelling upper extension. Standard CCE-on-initial-fluid generates the lower BOPVT table for p ≤ psi, leaving a missing GOR range from ~500 scf/STB to ~36,350 scf/STB that gas-injection cells routinely traverse and that triggers simulator instability. The paper closes the gap with an upper table built via incremental swelling of the initial-fluid equilibrium liquid with the injection gas to a critical mixture (Ki = 1), running a CCE at each swell increment between maximum pressure and the cell's saturation pressure. Of three tested extension methods (single-stage swell, incremental swell, convergence-pressure negative-flash), incremental swell is selected for stability and smooth GOR / Rs / 1/rs transitions.
  • F2F operational design rule pwf < MMPMC < pfr < ptwf. F2F succeeds only if the frontal pressure pfr stays above MMPMC for a robust majority of the production period. Recipe: hold injection-fracture BHP at the MMPMC-derived minimum (4500 psia in the worked example) during production and fix the production-period duration for all subsequent cycles equal to that of the first cycle. Three contributing factors maintain miscibility: (1) the displacement front stays close to the injection fracture so pfr ≈ pinj-fr; (2) miscibility is a dimmer not a switch (near-miscible pfr is still highly efficient); (3) constant-duration production schedules cause the minimum injection-fracture pressure to increase with cycle, adding pressure to the front.
  • F2F BOPVT validity by fluid type. For black-oil / volatile-oil / near-critical-oil with Rs < 2000 scf/STB, BOPVT and COMP agree well on F2F oil recovery: most of the pressure drop occurs in the oil phase ahead of the front and the mixing zone stays narrow, so the static 1/rs(p) approximation is locally valid. For near-critical gas condensate and gas condensate, the cyclic pressure swing causes pfr to oscillate around MMPMC and gas can cyclically move ahead of the front and disperse the miscible bank. Recovery degenerates to vaporisation-driven (which BOPVT cannot capture) plus partially-miscible Buckley-Leverett. NCGC breakthrough occurs much later in BOPVT than COMP; GC F2F is essentially vaporisation-only and BOPVT fails entirely.
  • Recurrently-changing BOPVT tables via CVI/CVD as 34-36x faster COMP alternative. The static-rs(p) failure can be patched by recurrently changing the BOPVT table - one table per HnP cycle - using Sensor's PVT-region update capability. Cycle-end matrix compositions can be obtained either from a one-off COMP simulation or, more cheaply, from the Carlsen-Whitson-Dahouk 2019 CVI/CVD experiment on a tuned EOS. Runtime on the VO single-layer HnP case: BOPVT single-table 10.6 min, BOPVT CVI/CVD multi-table 10.6 min, BOPVT COMP-zi multi-table 11.2 min, COMP 382.1 min - 34-36x speedup with substantially improved accuracy over single-table BOPVT, useful when many runs are needed in a short time (e.g. history matching).

Significance and Contribution

The BOPVT-vs-COMP question, settled by fluid type and process. The 2020 unconventional-EOR cluster needed an answer to a question every operator runs into: can we keep using black-oil tables for HnP and F2F, or do we have to switch to compositional? This paper says: BOPVT systematically overpredicts HnP across all fluid types (use COMP or the CVI/CVD multi-table patch); BOPVT works for F2F on low-to-medium-GOR oils but fails for gas-condensate systems where miscibility is fragile.

The upper-extension BOPVT table. The simulator-instability problem when gas-injection cells traverse the missing GOR range above psi is a well-known practical headache. The incremental-swelling extension named in this paper is a concrete recipe that avoids the instability and preserves smooth lookups. The choice between three extension methods - and the argument for incremental-swelling - is the kind of engineering judgement that makes the paper operationally usable.

The 34-36x COMP-speedup patch. The CVI/CVD-driven recurrently-changing BOPVT tables sit at the right place on the cost / accuracy curve for history-matching workflows where many runs are needed. The matrix-composition source matters less than that some reasonable composition is fed in per cycle. The patch makes HnP simulation tractable for ensemble forecasting.
