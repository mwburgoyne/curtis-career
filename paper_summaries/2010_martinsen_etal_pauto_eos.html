Advanced Fluid Characterisation of Pauto Complex, Colombia

Authors: Sissel Ø. Martinsen, I. Castiblanco, R. Osorio, Curtis H. Whitson (PERA, BP Exploration Colombia, ECOPETROL, NTNU)

Venue: SPE Annual Technical Conference and Exhibition, Florence, 19-22 September 2010

Paper number: SPE-135085

Citations (OpenAlex): 8

EOS Tuning Pauto Field Colombia PVT QC Workflow Non-Exponential C7+ BIP Regression Multi-Lab Data Phase 3

Context

Case-history EOS characterisation of the Pauto Field Complex in the Andean Foothills basin, Colombia: eight compartmentalised gas-condensate and volatile-oil reservoirs, gas-oil ratio 30-350 bbl/MMscf, depth 12,000-16,000 ft, initial gas in place over 1 trillion cubic feet. Operated by BP with ECOPETROL. A single 12-component Peng-Robinson 1979 EOS (EOS12-2008) is fit to 11 samples from 8 reservoirs including one multi-contact swelling (MCV) study and multi-laboratory PVT data with identifiably erroneous Z-factors and material balances. The EOS uses Søreide C7+ characterisation, Jhaveri-Youngren volume translation, and Lohrenz-Bray-Clark viscosity with critical Z-factor tuning. Supersedes the earlier EOS12-2006 (depletion-only tuning) with vaporisation-tuned binary interaction parameters.

Key Contributions

  • Systematic PVT-data QC workflow for EOS development. Before tuning the EOS, lab data pass three independent checks:
    1. Z-factor consistency via Hall-Yarborough plus Wichert-Aziz. Compute Z from the measured equilibrium gas composition with Hall-Yarborough 1973 (matches Standing-Katz to 2-3%). Compare against lab-reported Z; mismatch above 5% at high pressure indicates lab error. Pauto finding: one of three laboratories reported Z-factors 5-10% high for samples 1, 2, 3, 6, 7, 8, 10 at pressures above 5,000 psia; these Z-factors were excluded from EOS regression. The other two labs matched within 3% and were retained.
    2. Forward material balance. Start with known initial charge composition, track through each experimental stage, compare with measured compositions. Mismatch flags lab error.
    3. Backward material balance. Start with final measured composition, work backwards via measured volumes to reconstruct the initial charge, compare with known charge. Pauto CVD sample 5 agreed to within 0.3 mol% across 11 components - a strong pass.
    Plus graphical cross-plots: liquid API vs C7+ gravity, saturation pressure vs C7+ mol fraction, saturated Bo vs solution GOR. A single "wrong" lab value can drive EOS regression in a non-physical direction; the QC workflow separates outlier detection from EOS tuning. In consulting practice, 10-30% of reported PVT data typically should be excluded from the tuning set.
  • Non-exponential C7-C12 multiplier extension of the gamma C7+ characterisation. Pauto fluids exhibit non-exponential behaviour in the C7-C12 single-carbon-number distribution that a pure gamma distribution cannot reproduce. Rather than increase the gamma shape parameter α (which compromises the heavy-tail fit), apply composition-specific multipliers mi for C7-C12: xi = mi · xiexp. Pauto multipliers (common to all 11 samples across 8 compartments): C7 = 0.70, C8 = 1.29, C9 = 1.13, C11 = 0.74, C12 = 0.71. The non-exponential pattern is a compositional fingerprint of source rock plus migration history, stable across compartments sharing a source. Captures the C7-C12 behaviour with fixed multipliers; keeps the gamma exponential tail for C13+ where it works well.
  • Pivot-shift critical-temperature regression formula preserves K-value monotonicity. Tc,ituned = fTc(Tc,iinitial - 950°R) + 950°R with fTc as a single dimensionless regression variable applied to all C7+ pseudo-components. The 950°R pivot is approximately the average critical temperature of a typical C7+ characterisation. Direct scaling Tctuned = f · Tcinitial distorts the critical-temperature spread unevenly across light and heavy fractions, breaking K-value monotonicity. The pivot-shift form preserves it.
  • K-value consistency check after extensive BIP regression. Extensive BIP regression (separate BIPs for many pseudo-component pairs) can produce non-monotonic BIPs with opposite signs between contiguous bands. EOS12-2008 has BIP(C1, C5-6) = +0.068 and BIP(C1, C7-10) = -0.179. Generally a warning sign of non-physical EOS behaviour. Mandatory check after extensive BIP regression: compute K-values over the relevant pressure range and verify (a) Ki monotonically decreases with molecular weight at fixed pressure, and (b) Ki(p) curves do not cross. For EOS12-2008, the K-values pass both consistency checks despite the non-monotonic BIPs. Non-monotonic BIPs are therefore admissible if the K-value check passes.

Significance and Contribution

The QC-before-regression discipline. The standard practice in the 1990s and 2000s was to accept lab-reported PVT data as ground truth and tune the EOS to match. This paper argues that 10-30% of reported PVT data should typically be excluded - and gives a workflow to identify which 10-30%. The Hall-Yarborough cross-check, forward and backward material balance, and graphical cross-plots are now standard pre-regression steps at Whitson AS and the operators that follow the framework.

The non-exponential-multiplier trick. Real reservoir C7+ distributions are not exact gamma. The multiplier approach handles the systematic non-exponential fingerprint (same across all samples in the reservoir complex) with fixed multipliers, while the gamma still governs C13+ where it works well. The 2024 Nielsen-Wanat-Whitson SPE-220699 paper picks up the same theme with the "adjust gamma lower MW" technique for legacy truncated samples.

The K-value-check guardrail. Curtis's framing: BIP-tuning-outcome QC trumps BIP-tuning-input constraints. Allow the regression broad freedom on BIPs, then check the outcome against thermodynamic requirements. The K-value consistency check is the right guardrail.
