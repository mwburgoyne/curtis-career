Miscible Gas Injection in Undersaturated Gas-Oil Systems

Authors: Lars Høier, N. Cheng, Curtis H. Whitson

Venue: SPE Annual Technical Conference and Exhibition, Houston, 26-29 September 2004

Paper number: SPE-90379

Citations: not catalogued

Undersaturated Gas-Oil Compositional Grading First-Contact Miscibility Leading-Edge Oil Bank System MMP Point Injection Smørbukk South Phase 3

Context

The Lars Høier - Curtis Whitson compositional-grading sequence continues. The 1998 SPE-49269 paper had introduced MMP variation with depth for saturated GOC reservoirs. The 2001 SPE-63085 paper formalised compositional-grading theory and practice. This 2004 paper extends the framework to undersaturated gas-oil systems where the column grades continuously from gas at the top through near-critical to oil at the bottom without a saturated GOC. Named candidate fields include Brent (UK), Smørbukk South (Norway), Birba (Oman), Cusiana (Colombia), Anschutz (USA), Bunduq (UAE), and Orocual (Venezuela). The principal worked example is Smørbukk South Garn 1-2 (Norwegian Sea).

Key Contributions

  • Undersaturated gas-oil system: definition and first-contact-miscibility guarantee. An undersaturated gas-oil system has a fluid column that grades from gas to oil through a near-critical transition without a saturated GOC. The transition fluid is a critical composition, not a two-phase interface. Reservoir pressure exceeds saturation pressure at every depth. Under these conditions, any neighbouring pair of fluids along the column is first-contact miscible with each other, provided reservoir pressure stays above the maximum column saturation pressure (the "undersaturated-GOC psat"). A gravity-stable updip displacement at full-pressure maintenance therefore propagates first-contact miscibility throughout the entire column. Near-100% 1-D oil recovery is achievable at all depths.
  • Leading-edge oil bank miscible displacement principle. Once an injection gas develops multicontact miscibility with some reservoir fluid and the displacement front carries a leading-edge oil bank, the oil bank first-contact miscibly displaces any downstream oil regardless of whether the downstream oil is directly miscible with the injection gas. Miscibility propagates through a column of differing fluids via the oil-bank relay. Compositional streamline simulation is recommended over finite-difference to avoid numerical-dispersion destruction of the oil bank.
  • System MMP for undersaturated gas-oil reservoirs equals the C/V MMP at the shallowest depth where C/V develops. The headline result. For an injection gas where the condensing/vaporising mechanism develops somewhere above the GOC, the system MMP is the C/V MMP at that shallowest depth, not the GOC saturation pressure. The Smørbukk South example: separator gas develops C/V between 3719 and 3810 m at C/V MMP around 359 bara, while the GOC saturation pressure is 389 bara. The system MMP is 359 bara, not 389 bara. Compositional simulations at 359 bara achieve essentially 100% 1-D oil recovery. CO2-enriched injection gas (15% CO2 added to native separator gas) further unlocks C/V at shallower depths and reduces system MMP to 320 bara.
  • Partial pressure maintenance caveat. Local depletion below initial saturation pressure flashes the downstream oil and changes its composition relative to the original undersaturated state. The leading-edge oil bank may then encounter a flashed composition with a different MMP. The simple "system MMP equals shallowest C/V MMP" rule breaks. Simulation evidence shows system MMP rising toward the GOC psat as more of the column drops below initial saturation pressure before sweep arrival.
  • Point injection at the shallowest C/V-MMP depth as the preferred IOR strategy. The strategy inverts conventional gravity-stable-top-crest injection. Inject at the depth where C/V miscibility is first unlocked, then let the leading-edge oil bank propagate downdip while the same mechanism propagates updip through the shallower gas column. Benefits: operating pressure at the system MMP (rather than the GOC psat), reduced pressure-maintenance burden, and minimum exposure to local psat breach. The Smørbukk South example saves roughly 30 bara of operating pressure compared with top-crest injection. If native separator gas does not develop C/V, enrich with CO2 or C3-C5 to unlock C/V at a depth in the gas zone, and inject there.

Significance and Contribution

MMP as a system property, not a pair-wise property. The deeper conceptual move. MMP traditionally describes a single fluid-fluid pair. Høier-Cheng-Whitson 2004 reframes it: in a compositionally graded reservoir under gas injection, the MMP that matters is the system MMP, defined by whether multicontact miscibility develops anywhere in the column and whether the resulting oil bank survives to downstream oils. The gas-injection design question becomes: does my injection gas unlock C/V somewhere in the column? If yes, the operating pressure may be well below the GOC psat.

Phase 3 mature compositional-grading. The Høier-Whitson trilogy (1998, 2001, 2004) is the methodological backbone of the Whitson group's compositional-grading practice. The 2004 paper is the most operationally consequential of the three because it ties the grading-physics result to a concrete IOR design strategy (point injection at the C/V depth).

The Fergusson Medal lineage. Høier and Whitson jointly received the 2001 SPE Cedric K. Fergusson Medal for the 1998 SPE-49269 paper. This 2004 paper is the continuation of that programme three years later.
