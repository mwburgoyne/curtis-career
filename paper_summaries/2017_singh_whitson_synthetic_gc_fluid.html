Context

Singh is Curtis's long-standing PhD co-author (2000 BO vs EOS, 2005 GOR-recovery, 2007 BOPVT consistency, 2008 layered pseudopressure, 2009 gas-injection IOR, 2011 Tunisia). This paper documents, for the first time in the open literature, a PERA commercial methodology refined over 15 years across 20+ commercial projects for designing synthetic gas condensate fluids that mimic reservoir PVT, viscosity, and interfacial-tension (IFT) behaviour over the full relevant pressure range, enabling large-quantity flow-test experiments using inexpensive, non-toxic laboratory-grade compounds.

Key Contributions

  • Three-to-four component synthetic fluid design with C1 plus light-intermediate alkane plus Diphenylmethane heavy aromatic. The systematic component-slate selection rule:
    • Methane C1 - always included; supplies the gas-phase volumetric and viscosity behaviour.
    • Light-intermediate alkane (C2 to C10) - one or two compounds - supplies the condensate-forming vapour-pressure intermediate behaviour.
    • Heavy aromatic - most often Diphenylmethane (DPH-C1, C13H12, M = 168.24, SRK Tc = 760 K, pc = 27.10 bar, ω = 0.481, shift s = 0.25578, Zc,vis = 0.24145, parachor 419.2) - supplies the heavy-end liquid-dropout and IFT contribution.
    Occasionally n-C9 or n-C10 substitutes for DPH-C1 if it gives a better match.
  • SRK EOS with zero binary interaction parameters as the engineered simplification. All synthetic fluids modelled with Soave-Redlich-Kwong EOS with all BIPs set to zero. Tolerable for synthetic mixtures (but not for reservoir fluids) because synthetic 3-4-component hydrocarbon-only mixtures are dominated by the C1-heavy-aromatic pair interaction, which zero-BIP SRK plus appropriate volume shift captures adequately. Zero BIPs eliminate an entire axis of EOS-tuning non-uniqueness, leaving only composition and synthetic-fluid temperature Tsyn as regression variables.
  • Triple pressure-dependent property design criterion. Per Fevang-Whitson 1996 SPERE steady-state flow theory, gas-condensate relative permeability krg is a function of the krg/kro ratio which is itself a pressure-dependent function of total flowing composition plus PVT properties Vro/Vt and μg/μo. With capillary-number dependency added for near-well high-velocity conditions, gas-oil IFT σgo also becomes pressure-dependent. The design target is therefore the triple pressure-dependency (μg/μo, Vro/Vt, σgo), not the raw PVT properties themselves. This is the central methodological insight - design for the dimensionless drivers of flow performance, not for absolute properties.
  • Twelve-fluid open-literature validation across the full range of gas-condensate behaviour. Lean (Arun 1.1%, Khuff 2.5%, SPE-56476 3-11%, NS-LGC 12%) to near-critical (NS-NCGC 45%); sweet to very sour (22% H2S Brazeau, 34% H2S plus 6.5% CO2 Caroline); low-T Khuff 104 °C to high-T Arun and North-Sea 163 °C; dewpoint 165-473 bar. Tsyn = 50 °C consistently produced the best match across all 12 cases, down-shifted from reservoir temperatures 85-163 °C. The down-shift enables low-cost, ambient-lab-equipment flow tests because high reservoir temperatures are not required to reproduce reservoir behaviour.

Significance and Contribution

The right target for synthetic-fluid design. Curtis's teaching pattern: when running flow tests on a surrogate fluid, the right design target is not "match the PVT exactly" but "match the dimensionless drivers of flow behaviour". The Fevang-Whitson 1996 three-region theory tells us those drivers are (μg/μo, Vro/Vt, σgo). Designing for the triple invariance gives a synthetic fluid that produces representative krg in flow tests at conditions far easier to maintain than reservoir.

Engineered simplification. The zero-BIP SRK choice is non-obvious - a reservoir engineer would assume BIP tuning is essential. The paper's justification: synthetic mixtures with 3-4 components do not need BIPs because the dominant interaction is well-captured by volume shift alone. Eliminating BIPs as a regression variable removes a degree of freedom that would otherwise generate non-unique fits. This is the right engineering discipline: simplify the model to the minimum that does the job.

Fifteen years of PERA practice into the open literature. The paper makes commercial methodology publicly available, with worked examples spanning 12 published reservoir fluids. The consistent Tsyn = 50 °C finding across all 12 cases is the kind of empirical anchor that only emerges from long-term practice. Worth noting in any future review of laboratory flow-test design for gas-condensate research.
