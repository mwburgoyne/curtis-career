Phase Behavior — SPE Monograph Volume 20

Authors: Curtis H. Whitson, Michael R. Brule

Venue: Society of Petroleum Engineers, Richardson, Texas. SPE Monograph Series Volume 20 (Henry L. Doherty Series), 2000

ISBN: 978-1-55563-087-4. ~240 pages, 9 chapters, 4 appendices

Citations: Thousands (Google Scholar). The most cited textbook in compositional reservoir engineering. 115 knowledge atoms extracted in CurtisClaw (ATM-0007 through ATM-0115 plus appendix atoms)

Why it matters: This is the textbook. Every NTNU petroleum-engineering MSc opens it. Every Whitson Academy course traces back to it. Every commercial PVT package developer (CMG WinProp, Schlumberger PVTi, Calsep PVTsim, KAPPA Emeraude, whitson+) implements its workflows. Every EOS-tuning engineer who has not read it directly has read someone who read it. The monograph consolidates twenty years of Whitson's published work, integrates Michelsen's computational thermodynamics, Riazi-Daubert and Twu characterisation, Lohrenz-Bray-Clark viscosity, Coats-Smart regression, Soreide-Whitson brine EOS, and Fevang-Whitson near-well gas-condensate flow into one coherent reference, with notation, recommended procedures, and example problems. Twenty-five years later it remains the standard.

Context

By the late 1990s the cubic-EOS framework had matured enough that practitioners needed one authoritative reference rather than a scattering of SPE papers, textbook chapters, and software manuals. Curtis was forty-four, professor at NTNU, had founded Whitson AS a decade earlier, and had accumulated a personal corpus of about a hundred papers on fluid characterisation, EOS development, phase-equilibrium algorithms, gas-condensate flow, and black-oil PVT. Michael Brule, then at Mobil, brought industrial-laboratory perspective on PVT measurement and the surface-process side of black-oil tables.

The SPE Henry L. Doherty Monograph Series invited a successor to John Lohrenz's 1962 Phase Behavior Monograph 1 and to Don Katz's Handbook of Natural Gas Engineering. The brief was synthesis. The result is a 240-page reference where every method named in the text is an industry method, every recommendation has a worked example, and every chapter ends with a procedure that can be followed step-by-step.

Chapter 2 — Volumetric and Phase Behaviour of Oil and Gas Systems

The thermodynamic foundation. Gibbs phase rule applied to reservoir fluids, multicomponent phase envelopes, cricondentherm and cricondenbar, retrograde condensation, and the five-category fluid classification (dry gas, wet gas, gas condensate, volatile oil, black oil) anchored on Tres against Tc and Tcdt with C7+ and GOR ranges. Closes with the saturated-reservoir sampling caution that every PVT engineer needs the first time they see an initial-pressure-equals-saturation-pressure result.

  • Five-category fluid classification with C7+ and GOR ranges (Cronquist 1973 extended)
  • Retrograde condensation requirements and the phase-envelope shift during gas-condensate depletion
  • Volatile vs black oil practical distinction via material-balance comparison, with the 100% to 50% shrinkage example

Chapter 3 — Gas and Oil Properties and Correlations

The longest chapter. Twenty-three atoms cover gas pseudocriticals (Sutton, Wichert-Aziz), Z-factor (Hall-Yarborough, Dranchuk-Abou-Kassem), gas viscosity (Lucas, Lee-Gonzalez-Eakin), oil bubblepoint (Standing, Lasater), oil density (Standing-Katz), oil viscosity (Chew-Connally, Lohrenz-Bray-Clark), surface tension (Weinaug-Katz), and K-values (Wilson, Whitson-Torp, Standing). The chapter is the reference book that engineers reach for when matching black-oil correlations against measured data; it is also the standard for the warnings (dead-oil viscosity extreme paraffinicity sensitivity, oil compressibility validity range, when LBC fails).

  • Sutton hydrocarbon-gas pseudocritical correlations and Wichert-Aziz non-HC correction
  • Lucas gas viscosity (recommended general use); Lee-Gonzalez-Eakin for sweet natural gas
  • Whitson-Torp generalised K-values with convergence pressure; Standing low-pressure K-values for separator calculations
  • Dead-oil viscosity sensitivity to paraffinicity (the warning that prevents bad EOS tunes)

Chapter 4 — Equation-of-State Calculations

The computational heart of the book. Fifteen atoms cover Peng-Robinson and SRK with Peneloux volume translation, Rachford-Rice with bounded solution, the negative flash from Whitson-Michelsen 1989, Michelsen phase stability testing, Z-factor root selection by normalised Gibbs energy, isothermal compositional grading with Gibbs-Duhem consistency, Coats-Smart five-parameter regression, and Michelsen's saturation-pressure algorithm. This chapter is the working reference for anyone writing or maintaining an EOS code.

  • PR and SRK equivalent for VLE with volume translation - the practical decision rule
  • Negative flash for continuity across phase boundaries (Whitson-Michelsen 1989)
  • Michelsen tangent-plane stability test and saturation-pressure algorithm
  • Coats-Smart five-parameter regression with 5 to 10% modification typical

Chapter 5 — Heptanes-Plus Characterisation

The matured presentation of the 1983 SPE-12233 framework. TBP distillation as gold standard, Watson K as QC tool, Soreide and Twu correlations for critical properties, Lee-Kesler and Kesler-Lee acentric factor, the Pedersen SRK alternative procedure, the recommended PR EOS C7+ procedure step-by-step, Coats pseudoisation, Chueh-Prausnitz BIPs for C1-C7+ pairs, expected accuracy of an untuned EOS, and the Whitson guideline on pseudocomponent count. Thirteen atoms.

  • Recommended PR EOS C7+ characterisation procedure (the procedure)
  • Twu perturbation-expansion correlations preferred over Riazi-Daubert above Tb = 730 K
  • Coats pseudoisation preserves mixture EOS constants (a and b) on grouping

Chapter 6 — Conventional PVT Measurements

What the lab actually does and how to read its report. Wellstream composition from bottomhole and recombined samples, multistage separator test as the basis for DLE conversion, CCE on oils and gas condensates including supersaturation in volatile oils, the Y function for smoothing sub-bubblepoint data, DLE procedure and the residual-oil-to-stock-tank-oil conversion (the most critical step), CVD procedure with liquid-dropout tail issues, the Cho et al. maximum-dropout correlation, the Whitson-Torp CVD material-balance consistency check, and how to correct CVD recoveries when initial pressure is above the dewpoint. Twelve atoms.

  • The DLE-to-stock-tank conversion is the most critical step (where most lab-report misreadings happen)
  • Whitson-Torp CVD material-balance consistency check - if the numbers don't close, the lab data are suspect
  • Match cumulative condensate recovery, not the liquid-dropout tail

Chapter 7 — Black-Oil PVT Formulations

How to compress an EOS into a simulator-ready PVT table. Traditional formulation with Rs and Bo, validity limits, the oil-shrinkage plot for volatility evaluation, the modified black-oil formulation with rs and Bgd for volatile oils and gas condensates, dry-gas FVF correction, MBO surface-gravity mixing, solution oil/gas ratio derivation, reservoir-phase compositions and K-values from MBO properties, MBO inflow performance, Borthne MBO material balance, Drohm-Goldthorpe corrected MBO partial-density formulation, and the explicit statement that compositional simulation is preferred over pseudo-PVT for gas injection. Eleven atoms.

  • Modified black-oil formulation for volatile oils and gas condensates - rs and Bgd introduced
  • Compositional simulation preferred over pseudo-PVT for gas injection (the recommendation that ends the argument)

Chapter 8 — Gas-Injection Processes

Miscibility and gas EOR. MMP definition for binary and multicomponent systems, critical tie-line criterion for developed multicontact miscibility, why pseudoternary diagrams cannot uniquely represent multicomponent miscibility, slim-tube as the standard MMP determination, vaporising-gas mechanism, the Zick condensing-vaporising mechanism that replaced traditional enriched-gas drive interpretation, multicell vaporisation model for calculating developed miscibility, swelling test as the primary multicontact PVT experiment, why Benham et al. charts overestimate methane dilution, gas cycling and N2 revaporisation of retrograde condensate, CO2 physical properties, Simon-Graue and Holm-Josendal CO2 MMP correlations, CO2 VLLE behaviour, and CO2 solubility in water and brine. Fifteen atoms.

  • Zick combined condensing/vaporising mechanism (Aaron Zick's 1986 paper given its proper place)
  • Critical tie-line criterion for developed multicontact miscibility
  • Holm-Josendal CO2 MMP via CO2 density

Chapter 9 — Water/Hydrocarbon Systems

The water chapter Curtis added because no other textbook had it consolidated. Methane solubility in pure water (Culberson-McKetta polynomial), Setchenow salting-out for brines, Rowe-Chou brine PVT, dissolved-gas corrections for water FVF and compressibility, Kestin water/brine viscosity, McKetta-Wehe water content in natural gas, Ramey IFT, the Soreide-Whitson PR EOS modification for water/hydrocarbon systems (Soreide-Whitson 1992 FPE built in), oil/gas/water equilibrium with Rsw depletion behaviour, hydrate crystal structures I and II, Katz K-value method for hydrate prediction, and Hammerschmidt and Nielsen-Bucklin hydrate inhibition equations. Twelve atoms.

  • Soreide-Whitson PR EOS modification for water/hydrocarbon systems - the standard brine EOS
  • Katz K-value method for hydrate prediction
  • Hammerschmidt and Nielsen-Bucklin hydrate inhibition equations

Appendices A — D

Appendix A gives property tables (SCN, pure component, non-HC BIPs) and units. The non-HC BIP recommendations for PR and SRK (CO2, N2, H2S with hydrocarbons) become the default in every commercial PVT package. Appendix B walks through full oil characterisation from C7+ data through the separator flash. Appendix C gives a step-by-step PR EOS flash including Michelsen stability test, gas-condensate EOS regression strategies, and how to evaluate multiple EOS characterisations in reservoir simulation. Appendix D covers laboratory PVT report interpretation, including undersaturated compressibility from log-log Bod plots, Standing's DLE-to-flash conversion, the residual vs stock-tank oil distinction, and the MBO property non-monotonicity that gas condensates with dropout tails exhibit. Nine atoms across the appendices.

Significance and Contribution

The standard reference. The most cited textbook in compositional reservoir engineering. Used in every NTNU petroleum-engineering MSc cohort since 2000. Required reading in equivalent programmes at Stanford, Texas A&M, Imperial College, Heriot-Watt, U Texas Austin, U Tulsa, Colorado School of Mines. The anchor of every Whitson Academy course. The reference manual on every consulting engineer's desk.

Implemented in every commercial PVT package. The Recommended PR EOS C7+ Characterisation Procedure (Section 5.5) is what CMG WinProp, Schlumberger PVTi, Calsep PVTsim, and KAPPA Emeraude all default to, often by direct page reference. The Soreide-Whitson brine EOS (Chapter 9) is the brine module in every reservoir simulator that handles compositional water. The Whitson-Torp CVD consistency check (Chapter 6) is the lab-data QC every PVT engineer runs before accepting a report. The Recommended Non-HC BIPs (Appendix A) populate the BIP matrix every time anyone opens a new EOS file.

Consolidated twenty years of Whitson's own work plus Michelsen and the broader community. The negative flash (Whitson-Michelsen 1989, Chapter 4), the C7+ characterisation (Whitson 1983, Chapter 5), the CVD evaluation (Whitson-Torp 1981, Chapter 6), the brine EOS (Soreide-Whitson 1992, Chapter 9), the three-region gas-condensate flow framework (Fevang-Whitson 1996, referenced in Chapter 7), the compositional grading work (Whitson-Belery 1994, Chapter 4) - all consolidated, with notation aligned, procedures unified, and examples worked. Beyond Whitson's own papers the monograph integrates Michelsen's stability and saturation-pressure algorithms, Coats-Smart regression, Lohrenz-Bray-Clark viscosity, Pedersen's SRK characterisation as an alternative procedure, Twu and Riazi-Daubert critical properties, Zick's miscibility mechanism, and the full body of conventional PVT and gas-injection practice.

The 2025 papers still cite Chapter 7. The Singh-Whitson-Nielsen BOPVT-comparison framework (SPE-225382), the Nielsen-Younus E2E fluid model development (SPE-225470), and the Khan et al. communicating-reservoirs paper (SPE-225405) all reference the monograph as foundational. Twenty-five years later it remains the citation that PVT papers anchor on.
