Implementation of Stream Management at South Natuna Sea Block B, Indonesia

Authors: H. Suryanto, J. D. Ekstrand (ConocoPhillips Indonesia); Curtis H. Whitson, K. Uleberg, F. Hoda (PERA a/s)

Venue: 29th Indonesian Petroleum Association Annual Convention, October 2003

Paper number: IPA03-E068

Citations: not catalogued

Stream Management Belanak BOz Conversion z-to-z Conversion Integrated Asset Modelling Petrostreamz Phase 3

Context

A case study from ConocoPhillips' South Natuna Sea Block B / Belanak FPSO development. The asset comprises 19 reservoir models, 4 EOS models, 12 PVT regions, and two Gas Sales Agreements to fulfil. The PVT challenge: aggregate streams from a heterogeneous portfolio of black-oil and compositional reservoir simulators and present them to downstream process simulators and crude-assay calculations as one consistent compositional flow. PERA's PSM (Petrostreamz Management) software was the deployment vehicle. Curtis is listed as PERA author; the paper documents two operational extensions of Curtis's earlier monograph-era frameworks that are not in the Monograph itself. (Note: Petrostreamz, founded by Curtis in 2006 for the Pipe-It workflow product, evolves from the PSM software described here. PERA is the original 1988 consulting firm later renamed Whitson AS.)

Key Contributions

  • BOz conversion: Split Factor Matrix for grid-level black-oil to compositional stream transfer. The problem is converting black-oil stream rates (qo, qg) back to compositional molar rates for process simulation or asset aggregation. The solution is a Split Factor Matrix S generated at each pressure node of the BO-PVT table such that the molar rate of component i is mi = qo · Soi + qg · Sgi. The factors come from the saturated-phase compositions and molar volumes at the local pressure: Soi = xi / vomolar, Sgi = yi / vgmolar. The conversion uses the Whitson-Brule Fg (or β) recombination physics that was baked into the BO tables; no new thermodynamic calculation is needed. Validated on a North Belut 5-layer, 5-fluid synthetic depletion case showing excellent agreement between direct Eclipse 300 compositional simulation and Eclipse 100 BO simulation followed by BOz conversion across C1, C2, C3C4, and C5+ molar rate trajectories over 20 years.
  • z-to-z conversion: gamma-distribution rediscretisation for cross-simulator stream transfer. When a stream passes from a reservoir simulator with 5-8 components to a process simulator with 15-18 components (or between any two simulators with different characterisations), the C7+ portion is converted in two steps. Step 1: fit a continuous gamma distribution (α, η, M7+) to the incoming C7+. Step 2: rediscretise to the output characterisation's MW bins by integrating the gamma PDF between bin boundaries. The total C7+ amount is preserved; the distribution shape is preserved as described by (α, η, M7+). The conversion uses the Whitson-Brule pseudoisation framing directly: any C7+ characterisation is a discretisation of a common continuous distribution, and conversion between characterisations is fit-then-rediscretise. No EOS flash is required.

Significance and Contribution

Operationalising the Monograph framework. The Whitson-Brule 2000 Monograph treats the Fg recombination and the gamma-distribution C7+ framework at the theoretical level. This paper turns them into per-pressure-node Split Factor Matrices and a fit/rediscretise procedure that runs at every timestep at every well-grid connection in a multi-field asset model. The bridge from theory to commercial-software implementation is the value-add.

The integrated-asset story. Without BOz, BO streams can only be aggregated as surface oil and surface gas volumes - not as component molar rates - which breaks downstream process simulation and crude-assay prediction. The Belanak asset (4 EOS models, 12 PVT regions, gas-injection scenarios) is the kind of complexity that motivates the framework. Curtis's interest in integrated-asset modelling, which becomes Petrostreamz / Pipe-It (founded 2006, later sold to Tieto in 2018), traces back to this Belanak deployment and parallel work.
