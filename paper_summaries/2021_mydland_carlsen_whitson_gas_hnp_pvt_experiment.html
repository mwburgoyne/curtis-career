Context

Focused formalisation paper extending the HnP PVT experiment proposed in Carlsen et al. 2019 URTeC-539. Where URTeC-539 introduced the hybrid CVI-CVD concept as one of seven contributions, URTeC-5016 dedicates eighteen pages to the experiment alone, adding the operational protocol, the EOR-efficiency KPI, and the mixing-vs-vaporisation decomposition needed for lab-to-field translation. Mydland-first-authored Whitson AS work; funded by Innovation Norway and the Norwegian Research Council; acknowledges Keong Aik Lau at Stratum Reservoir for discussions.

Key Contributions

  • Gas HnP EOR-efficiency KPI EHnP in STB/MMscf. Lab-measurable performance indicator defined via a simplified surface process (C6+ components → STO, lighter → surface gas) with closed-form equations. The inverse 1 / EHnP in Mscf/STB is the "gas EOR utilisation factor" communicating incremental gas cost per incremental barrel for operator economics. Three-regime quantitative scaling for a Permian-representative oil with a 65% C1 / 20% C2 / 15% C3 rich injection gas at 200 °F: ~26 STB/MMscf pure-vaporisation; 60-120 STB/MMscf mixed-mechanism; 350-330 STB/MMscf pure-mixing. Gas condensate yields ~55% less than oil under pure-mixing conditions due to ~58% less C6+ target per unit pore volume from higher FVF. Cross-fluid-type comparison requires a total-FVF Bt saturation-weighted harmonic average.
  • Two-stage CVD puff protocol with formal pressure-stepping rules and MB QC framework. Formalises the CVI-CVD hybrid for lab implementation. Initialisation: CVD from pRi down to pmin (sets the cycle-1 starting state). Huff: CVI to pmax. Puff: two-stage CVD back to pmin - stage 1 at psat* (if pmax > psat*) or at (pmax + pmin) / 2 (if pmax ≤ psat*), stage 2 at pmin. Recommend 3-6 lab cycles with EOS extrapolation for further cycles. Forward (Whitson-Torp 1983) and backward (Whitson-Brule 2000) material-balance QC adapted to HnP, with K-value non-crossing consistency check.
  • Relative-uplift-from-mixing Rmix,l cycle-by-cycle decomposition. Splits per-cycle surface-oil production into the single-phase-mixing component (CVD stages where pcell,i ≥ psat*) and the vaporisation component (stages where pcell,i < psat*). Rmix,l is 100% if all stages are above psat*, 0% if all below; it decreases from near-100% at cycle 1 toward 0% as the fluid dilutes and psat* tracks upward into the cycling interval. Quantitatively grounds the three-scenario classification from Carlsen-Whitson 2019.

Significance and Contribution

From "this should work" to "here is the protocol". The 2019 URTeC-539 paper proposed the hybrid CVI-CVD experiment; this paper turns the proposal into a step-by-step lab procedure with pressure-stepping rules, surface-process definition, KPI calculation, and material-balance QC. The eighteen pages of pressure-stepping detail and MB QC are what lets a PVT lab actually run the experiment without re-deriving everything.

The three-regime scaling number is operationally load-bearing. Operators screening HnP gas selection now have a defensible scaling for the maximum achievable EHnP at each fluid type and pressure regime. The 26 / 60-120 / 350-330 STB/MMscf ladder lets economic models bracket the upside before the pilot is even funded.

The mixing-vs-vaporisation decomposition makes the diagnostic operational. Carlsen-2020-URTeC-2869 showed the wellstream-C7+ signature distinguishes mixing-driven from displacement-driven HnP in the field; this paper provides the lab-side companion that quantifies the mixing fraction cycle by cycle, closing the lab-to-field interpretation loop.
