Compositional Tracking of a Huff-n-Puff Project in the Eagle Ford: A Second Take

Authors: Mathias Lia Carlsen, Stian Mydland, Mohamad Majzoub Dahouk, Curtis H. Whitson, Ilina Yusra, M. Thuesen, R. Ambrose, J. Nohavitza (Whitson AS / NTNU / EP Energy)

Venue: Unconventional Resources Technology Conference, Austin, 20-22 July 2020

Paper number: URTeC-2869

DOI: 10.15530/urtec-2020-2869

Citations (OpenAlex (DOI)): 20

HnP Second Take Gas Condensate Area Wellstream C7+ Signature SV + Diffusion Validation Eagle Ford EP Energy Temperature-MMP Inversion Phase 4

Context

Follow-up to Carlsen-Whitson 2019 URTeC-539 (HnP compositional tracking in the Eagle Ford black oil area) applied to a separate Eagle Ford HnP field project in the gas condensate area (Rsi 3000-5000 scf/STB). Same Whitson AS plus EP Energy plus NTNU collaboration. Same HnP operational parameters (15 cycles, 15-45 day injection duration, 14-16 MMscf/D, 200-600 MMscf per cycle, injection gas 75% C1 / 13% C2 / 5% C3 / 7% C4+).

Key Contributions

  • Wellstream C7+ signature as HnP process-efficiency diagnostic. Time-resolved wellstream C7+ during the puff cycle distinguishes mixing-driven from displacement-driven HnP. Efficient mixing signature: C7+ starts at 0.5-1 mol% then decreases as BHP declines, matching DP simulation predictions and the SV-plus-diffusion premise. Inefficient piston-like displacement signature: C7+ rises from the injection-gas composition back toward the pre-implementation composition, matching SP planar-fracture simulation. The field measurement becomes a direct go/no-go test for whether HnP is delivering on its physical premise.
  • Gas-condensate area vs black-oil area HnP recovery-efficiency ordering. Gas-condensate-area wellstream C7+ during HnP is 0.25-1 mol%; black-oil-area wellstream C7+ is ~2 mol%. Lower recovery efficiency in the gas-condensate area is not due to a worse process - it is due to a smaller EOR target volume. Oil reservoirs with Boi < 2 (Rsi < 1000 scf/STB) have the largest relative EOR target and the highest sweep efficiency.
  • Field validation of the SV / rubble hypothesis. The wellstream-C7+ signature from this field matches DP simulation predictions and contradicts SP planar-fracture predictions. Combined with Raterman et al. 2017/2019 ConocoPhillips Eagle Ford and Gale et al. 2018 HFTS Wolfcamp physical evidence, this converts the shattered-volume hypothesis (Mydland-Yusra-Whitson-Dahouk-Carlsen 2020) from premise to field-validated fact for modern Eagle Ford MFHW completions.
  • Temperature-MMP inversion between conventional displacement and HnP. Conventional displacement: MMPMC is proportional to temperature (higher T → worse displacement). HnP: MMPFC is inversely proportional to temperature (higher T → better mixing). Swell-test-based MMP characterisation. Counter-intuitive operational implication: higher-temperature reservoirs favour HnP but disfavour conventional miscible flooding - the same reservoir-T axis that hurts conventional EOR helps cyclic-same-well EOR.
  • Three operational characteristics of a successful gas-HnP project. (1) large injection volumes; (2) rapid large pressure buildup indicating good conformance; (3) wells with modern completions creating large surface area via shattering. Plus flagged-as-opinion operational recommendations on containment, communication, fracture gradient, and infill drilling - the Whitson-AS practice notes that have accumulated across the Eagle Ford pilot fleet.

Significance and Contribution

From hypothesis to field-validated diagnostic. The shattered-volume premise was a modelling-side argument in the May-2020 mechanisms paper; this July-2020 paper carries it through to a real field signature on a real well. The wellstream-C7+ diagnostic is now operationally usable: any operator running an HnP cycle can pull the puff-cycle compositional data and ask whether the signature matches mixing or piston-like behaviour.

The temperature-MMP inversion is a thermo-physical observation with screening consequences. Operators screening unconventional plays for HnP can use reservoir temperature as a favouring rather than disfavouring axis, which inverts the conventional miscible-flood intuition. Same play, same gas, same MMP measurement methodology - opposite direction of preferability.

Black-oil-area is the largest EOR target. Naming the Boi < 2 / Rsi < 1000 scf/STB threshold for largest relative EOR target gives operators a coarse screening axis for which wells in a mixed-fluid play (Bakken oil window, Eagle Ford volatile / black oil transition) deserve HnP pilots first.
