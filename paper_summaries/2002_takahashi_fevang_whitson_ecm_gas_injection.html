Fluid Characterisation for Gas Injection Study using Equilibrium Contact Mixing

Authors: K. Takahashi (BUNDUQ Co. Ltd.), Øivind Fevang (PERA), Curtis H. Whitson (NTNU / PERA)

Venue: 10th Abu Dhabi International Petroleum Exhibition and Conference, 13-16 October 2002

Paper number: SPE-78483

Citations: not catalogued

ECM Gas Injection Compositional Gradient Multi-Sample EOS Near-Critical Abu Dhabi MCV Tuning Phase 3

Context

A compositionally graded near-critical oil reservoir in Abu Dhabi where no representative gas-cap sample was available. Twenty fluid samples from 13 wells; 36 CCE, 18 DLE, 60 SEP, 4 multi-contact swelling (MCV), 3 slimtube tests. The PVT challenge: tune one EOS for the entire column from oil-zone samples alone, then predict the gas cap. The paper extends the Fevang-Whitson 1994 SPE-28829 ECM construction from cross-sample reconstruction to a missing-gas-cap synthesis problem, and quantifies how much the missing-sample correction matters.

Key Contributions

  • ECM test as workaround for missing gas-cap sample. Procedure: (1) collect separator samples from an oil well producing with gas coning; (2) recombine separator liquid and vapour roughly 50/50 by volume to produce a recombined fluid representing the GOC-region mixture; (3) perform a full-volume PVT equilibration (24 hours agitation plus settle) to produce equilibrium gas-phase and equilibrium oil-phase samples; (4) measure composition and PVT of both equilibrium phases separately; (5) use the ECM equilibrium data as "representative" GOC data for EOS regression. The EOS is then tuned so that an isothermal compositional-gradient calculation from an oil-zone sample reproduces the ECM equilibrium phases at the GOC depth, tying the EOS to the near-critical transition behaviour without an actual gas-cap sample.
  • Multi-sample single-EOS Gaussian-quadrature framework. Extension of the 1989 Whitson-Anderson-Søreide approach. For sample j the pseudo-fraction i mole fraction is zi(j) = zC7+(j) · Wi · f(Xi(j)). Fixed across samples: the 5-node Gaussian quadrature abscissae and weights, shape parameter α, node count. Variable per sample: η(j), MN(j), and the measured C7+ composition. All samples share the same physical pseudocomponent set with different proportions. Abu Dhabi parameters: α = 1.7, η = 90, MN = 500; final 16-component EOS matched 20 samples within 0.5% of measured saturation pressures.
  • Representative-sample selection via trend-match against observed depth distributions. After tuning the multi-sample EOS, compute a compositional-gradient prediction from each sample's composition as anchor at its sampling depth. The sample whose prediction best reproduces the observed depth cloud of ps, GOR, C1, C7+, MC7+, and H2S becomes the "most representative" sample for GOC prediction. The empirical-representativeness reframe: a sample earns the label by winning the trend-match, not by being intrinsically more reliable. Decision rule for ties: weight ps-vs-depth most heavily.
  • Multi-contact swelling tests with multiple injection gases are essential for near-critical tuning. CCE, CVD, DLE probe single-composition behaviour at different pressures. They do not probe the composition-varying path the reservoir traverses during gas injection. For near-critical fluids the EOS regressed against depletion data alone misses the critical transition. The Abu Dhabi practice used 4 MCV tests from different wells with different injection gases; the initial depletion-only EOS under-predicted MCV saturation pressures by 8-15% at moderate injection-gas ratios. The final EOS regressed against depletion plus MCV matches all 4 MCV experiments within ±3%. Multiple injection gases (not just one) are needed because each gas probes a different direction of the near-critical composition-pressure surface.
  • ECM data materially enriches predicted gas-cap C7+ composition. The quantification for Abu Dhabi: without ECM, the EOS predicts gas-cap C7+ at 4.54 mol% and C1 at 62.04%. With ECM, the same column gives C7+ at 8.60% and C1 at 56.02%. The gas-cap classification shifts from lean to medium-rich. The bias direction is systematic - depletion-only EOS tuning under-predicts gas-cap C7+ richness. Downstream economic consequence: an under-rich gas-cap assumption over-predicts oil vaporisation, over-states miscibility, and understates remaining oil-in-place. For near-critical compositionally-graded reservoirs without a gas-cap sample, ECM is not an academic exercise; it materially changes the engineering conclusion.

Significance and Contribution

ECM lives. The 1994 Fevang-Whitson SPE-28829 paper introduced the Equilibrium Contact Mixing framework for sample reconstruction in saturated reservoirs. This 2002 paper extends the framework to a different problem (synthesise missing gas-cap data) and demonstrates that the framework is robust enough to support an operator-scale gas-injection study where direct sampling was infeasible.

The multi-sample single-EOS principle becomes the field-wide-EOS-development standard. Combined with the Orocual 2000 SPE-63088 work, this 2002 paper anchors the position that one EOS describes one continuous fluid system, regardless of compositional gradients, near-critical transitions, or compartmental complications. The principle is now the spine of the Whitson AS workflow.

The MCV recommendation is operationally consequential. Curtis's framing: CCE tells you what happens at constant composition; MCV tells you what happens when the composition changes. Near the critical point, the composition-change physics dominates, and EOS tuning without MCV data systematically misses by 10% or more on saturation pressures along the gas-injection path. This finding still drives Whitson AS recommendations for gas-injection PVT programmes.
