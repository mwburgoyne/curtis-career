Depletion Oil Recovery for Systems with Widely Varying Initial Composition

Authors: Kameshwar Singh, Øivind Fevang, Curtis H. Whitson

Venue: Journal of Petroleum Science and Engineering, Vol. 46, pp. 283-297 (2005)

DOI: 10.1016/j.petrol.2005.01.008

Originally: 10th India Oil and Gas Review Symposium, Mumbai, 8-9 September 2003

Citations: 6

Context

A systematic depletion-recovery study across a continuous GOR sweep from 35 to 25,000 standard m3/standard m3 (Sm3/Sm3), generated by recombining separator samples of a critical reservoir mixture to different gas-oil ratios. The fluid spectrum covers low-GOR black oil through volatile oil and the critical point at about 700 Sm3/Sm3 to lean gas condensate. A single black-oil PVT table (Peng-Robinson EOS with five C7+ fractions, critical point 510 bar / 130°C) anchors all fluids. Reservoir conditions: porosity 0.30, permeability 5 millidarcy, layer thickness 30 m, initial reservoir pressure 517 bar. The paper maps recovery across the full fluid taxonomy and identifies four sensitivities to relative permeability, critical gas saturation, plateau-rate constraints, and grid resolution. The companion paper to Fevang-Singh-Whitson 2000 SPE-63087: that paper addressed the BO-vs-compositional model choice for a given fluid; this paper maps recovery vs initial composition across the full fluid taxonomy.

Key Contributions

  • Non-monotonic depletion recovery across the fluid spectrum with a ~16% minimum at the critical point. Ultimate stock-tank-oil (STO) recovery vs initial solution GOR Rsi traces a U-shape with four regimes: rising from low-GOR oils to a maximum of ~30-40% at Rsi ≈ 100 Sm3/Sm3; falling through volatile oil and near-critical to a minimum of about 16% STO recovery at the critical mixture (Rsi ≈ 700 Sm3/Sm3); the same 16% floor on the gas-condensate side; then rising monotonically as condensate becomes lean toward 92% surface-gas recovery at Rsi = 25,695 Sm3/Sm3. The mechanism: at the critical mixture, the fluid is saturated at initial reservoir pressure (no undersaturation expansion), has near-neutral compressibility, and exhibits the highest retrograde liquid saturation with immobile condensate. The number to remember: 16% STO recovery at the critical mixture, versus 30-40% best-case oil and over 90% lean gas condensate.
  • Gas-oil relative permeability sensitivity is sharply localised. Relative-permeability uncertainty affects ultimate recovery 30-40% on the moderate-GOR oil side (at Rsi ≈ 100 Sm3/Sm3) but has essentially zero effect on gas-condensate recovery. The physical reason: condensate recovery is governed by phase behaviour and CVD-style depletion, not by retrograde liquid mobility, because the condensate stays immobile over practically the entire depletion history. Operational consequence: SCAL programmes that measure gas-oil relative permeability for reserves purposes are first-order on oil assets, but essentially pointless for condensate recovery quantification.
  • Plateau-rate-constrained optimum GOR shifts upward from the ultimate-recovery optimum. When a producer is subject to a constant-rate plateau for a fixed period (5 years used as reference), the GOR maximising plateau oil rate is Rsi ≈ 200-250 Sm3/Sm3 - higher than the ultimate-recovery optimum at 100 Sm3/Sm3. Plateau oil rate halves as GOR rises from 250 to 700 Sm3/Sm3. Plateau gas rate increases monotonically with GOR across the 700-25,000 condensate range. The two optima differ because reserves and rate are not the same thing: the rate-optimal fluid is richer than the recovery-optimal oil, because solution-gas expansion above the bubblepoint supports high early rates.
  • Coarse-grid plateau-period overprediction: about 40% for gas condensates, about 20% for moderate-GOR oils, negligible for near-critical. A typical full-field coarse-grid Cartesian model (30-90 m areal grids, 30 m thick) with standard well-productivity treatment (no pseudopressure, no blockage skin) overpredicts plateau duration by 40% for gas condensates with Rsi = 1,000-10,000 Sm3/Sm3, by about 20% for moderate-GOR oils at 100-200 Sm3/Sm3, and not at all for near-critical or volatile oils. The error arises from numerical dispersion and smeared near-wellbore pressure gradients that under-estimate the intensity of condensate blockage in the gas-condensate region. Remedies: pseudopressure well treatment, constant blockage-skin surrogate, or local grid refinement around each well.
  • Critical gas saturation controls oil depletion recovery but has no effect on gas-reservoir recovery. Varying critical gas saturation Sgc across 0.0-0.20 shifts oil STO recovery from about 33% to 38% near the optimum (Rsi ≈ 100 Sm3/Sm3) but has no detectable effect on condensate recovery for Rsi > 700 Sm3/Sm3. SCAL programmes measuring Sgc for reserves quantification are valuable on oil assets and essentially pointless on gas-condensate assets. (For deliverability, Sgc still matters via its effect on krg/kro - a separate concern from recovery.)

Significance and Contribution

The universal recovery map. The U-shaped curve becomes the canonical depletion-screening tool across the reservoir-fluid classification. The practical question that follows is always "on which side of the minimum does my reservoir sit?", which makes the saturation-pressure-vs-GOR diagnostic the entry point for any depletion-vs-IOR decision. Reservoirs with psat close to initial reservoir pressure are at or near the 16% minimum and need careful recovery-mechanism assessment.

Where SCAL pays. The relative-permeability and critical-gas-saturation sensitivity maps reframe SCAL priorities. For oil assets at moderate GOR (50-250 Sm3/Sm3), gas-oil kr is exactly where the reserves-uncertainty money should go. For gas condensates, kr measurements are deliverability-relevant (blockage) but not recovery-relevant. For oils far from the GOR peak, other uncertainties pay more than relative permeability.

The 40% rule for gas-condensate coarse-grid screening. Whitson's teaching shorthand: if you look at a coarse-grid full-field plateau-length prediction for a gas condensate without pseudopressure or blockage-skin treatment, mentally discount the number by 40% before using it for contract-quota design.
