Fractional RTA Acuña δ-Parameter LFP' Generalisation Distribution of Fragments NRTA Part 1 Theory and Procedure Phase 4

Context

Theoretical / procedural companion to SPE-210420-MS (Carlsen-Whitson 2022 Part 2, the practical-usage and field-application paper). Part 1 introduces fractional RTA theory and the modified 13-step Numerical RTA workflow for uneven-fracture-spacing fracture-swarm geometries; Part 2 carries the theory through to simulated and SPE-data-repository field examples including the Crow well. Both papers come out of the Whitson AS Numerical RTA JIP and were presented at separate conferences (URTeC June 2022, SPE ATCE October 2022) within a year of SPE-205884's launch of the all-fluid NRTA workflow.

Key Contributions

  • Fractional-RTA fundamentals. The rate-normalised-pressure power-law signature Δp / q = m t1-δ + b on a log-log plot, with δ ∈ [0, 0.5] the Acuña fracture-spacing-heterogeneity parameter. Classical RTA is the δ = 0.5 evenly-spaced-fracture special case; δ = 0 is the pure fracture-swarm limit. The framework is the natural generalisation of Wattenbarger-style linear flow.
  • Generalised LFP' geometric form. LFP' = A kδ φ1-δ = 4 nf h xf kδ φ1-δ, paired with the saturation-weighted total formation volume factor Bti introduced in SPE-205884 for all-fluid handling. Recovers the SPE-205884 classical workflow exactly at δ = 0.5.
  • Distribution-of-fragments reconstruction procedure (Eqs 5-9, Table 1). Closed-form recipe to convert (δ, Smin, Smax, m) into a fragment-size distribution {Si, ni} with ratio R = exp(ln(Smax/Smin) / (m-1)) and mass-balanced fragment counts. Enables simulation of fracture-swarm networks via m independent symmetry-element models rather than a single template fracture.
  • Modified 13-step NRTA workflow for uneven fracture spacing. Extends the SPE-205884 eight-step workflow to handle the fractional-δ case: extract δ from the power-law fit; construct the fragment distribution; run m symmetry-element template models; combine into the well-scale type curves; pick representative LFP' and OOIP. The procedure cleanly decomposes geometry assumptions from PVT / saturation / relperm inputs so the same machinery serves classical and fractional cases.
  • Appendix A "Cheat Sheet for Engineers". Fifty-three equations spanning DCA, classical and fractional RTA, producing GOR, water cut, Darcy, compressibility, superposition, material balance, and diffusivity. Same reference-table appendix as Part 2; appears in both papers as the consolidated engineer-facing tool.

Significance and Contribution

Theory first, application second. The Whitson-group pattern of pairing a theory paper with a practical-usage paper recurs here. Part 1 lays out the fundamentals and the procedure; Part 2 carries it through to the Crow-well reconciliation and the seven-regime flow classification. Operators wanting the recipe read Part 1; operators wanting the example read Part 2.

The δ parameter as bridge between Acuña fractal-network theory and operator RTA practice. Acuña's fractal-network framework had been in the literature since 2016 but had not made the jump into routine rate-transient analysis. Embedding δ in the existing LFP'-based NRTA workflow brings it into operational reach.

The construction procedure makes fracture-swarm simulation tractable. Without the closed-form fragment-distribution recipe, simulating an uneven-fracture-spacing well would require many ad-hoc symmetry-element configurations. Equations 5-9 fix that with one consistent construction tied to the measured δ.
