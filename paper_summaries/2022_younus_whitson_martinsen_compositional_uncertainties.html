Compositional Uncertainties in Laboratory PVT Data

Authors: Bilal Younus, Curtis H. Whitson, Sissel Ø. Martinsen (Whitson AS / NTNU)

Venue: ADIPEC, Abu Dhabi, UAE, 2022

Paper number: SPE-211391-MS

DOI: 10.2118/211391-MS

Citations: 4

Lab PVT QC GC Compositional Uncertainty Baseline Shift Internal Standard Gamma-Model QC Cx Diagnostic Plot Duvernay Field-Wide EOS Phase 4

Context

Methodology paper introducing a systematic, quantitative procedure to identify and correct individual-sample laboratory C7+ compositional errors using an already-developed field-wide gamma distribution model. Where Younus et al. 2019 URTeC-551 covered the development of a field-wide gamma model, this 2022 ADIPEC paper addresses the QC of new samples against that model. Validated on 50+ public Duvernay-basin PVT samples spanning a decade across several PVT labs. The paper's own claim: "to our knowledge, this is the first attempt to identify and deal with composition errors with a systematic and comprehensive workflow."

Key Contributions

  • Four-source GC compositional-uncertainty taxonomy. (a) Baseline shift (BS) in gas chromatography; (b) internal standard (IS) error; (c) component molecular-weight error; (d) GOR-recombination error. Composite effect: ±0.2-2 mol% errors in methane and C7+ per Mawlod et al. 2021. Formal error definitions: EIS = Atlab / Atcorrect - 1; EBS as a sliding-baseline area offset; parameter Cx = the first SCN fraction where errors begin (typically beyond C15).
  • Gamma-model GC QC workflow with Cx diagnostic plot. Procedure using the field-wide gamma model (fixed α, η, MLi) with sample-specific M7+ and Cx+ multiplier as two regression variables. Starts with Cx = CN+, classifies the sample as (i) no-error, (ii) IS-only (mismatch only in CN+), or (iii) BS-only-or-combined (mismatch for some Cx < CN+). The Cx diagnostic plot of C15- deviation plus total calculated mass vs trial Cx identifies the correct Cx as the heaviest SCN with minimum C15- deviation; the total-calculated-mass value at that Cx distinguishes BS-only (≈ 1) from combined IS+BS (≠ 1).
  • Post-QC sample-specific EOS-tuning variables VM7+ and VN+. Two regression knobs introduced after the gamma-model QC. VM7+ on C7+ average MW impacts the mass distribution of all C7+ components; VN+ is a multiplier on the CN+ amount (mass or mole). Typical VM7+ adjustment under 5% for good-quality samples; can be larger for flagged samples. Allows iterative EOS tuning where sample-specific uncertainties in C15- distribution and the CN+ integration-to-infinity residual are absorbed by these two knobs rather than perturbing the field-wide gamma parameters.

Significance and Contribution

Lab-QC at scale via the field-wide model. The 2019 URTeC-551 field-wide EOS gave operators a single basin-scale fluid model; this paper closes the loop by giving them a quantitative tool to QC each new sample's GC against that model. Outliers are classified into IS, BS, or combined errors with the Cx diagnostic plot, and the two-knob EOS-tuning recipe absorbs residual sample-specific uncertainty without compromising the field-wide gamma framework.

Named errors are addressable errors. Curtis's pattern across forty years: define the failure mode, name its parameters, give the diagnostic plot, then the recipe. Same approach as the 1983 SPE-12233 C7+ characterisation, the 1996 Fevang-Whitson three-region GCB, and the 2000 monograph's PVT-data QC chapters. The four-source taxonomy makes lab-side error attribution tractable for operators who previously had no way to challenge a vendor's GC report.

Duvernay validation across labs and across a decade. 50+ public samples, several PVT labs, ten-year span. That breadth of validation makes the workflow defensible across the unconventional fleet rather than being a one-basin trick.
